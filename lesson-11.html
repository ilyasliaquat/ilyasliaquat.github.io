Understanding Sukoon and Jazm

What is Jazm?

Jazm ( ْ ) is a diacritical mark in the Arabic script that represents Sukoon, meaning the absence of a vowel sound on a consonant. It indicates that the letter should be pronounced without any Harakah (movement).

What is a Letter with a Jazm Called?

A letter that carries the Jazm is called a Sakin Letter (حرف ساكن) because it is silent and does not have a vowel sound.

How Many Times is That Letter Pronounced?

A letter with a Jazm is pronounced only once, with a short and quick sound, without elongation.

Distinguishing Between Similarly Pronounced Letters

It is important to learn and recognize the difference between Arabic letters that have similar pronunciations but distinct sounds. Here are some commonly confused letters: ظ (Zha) vs. ز (Zay) vs. ذ (Dhal) ص (Sad) vs. س (Seen) vs. ث (Tha) Proper pronunciation of these letters helps in correct Quranic recitation and Arabic language proficiency.
