Noorani Qaida Lesson-4: The Movements (Harakat)

Introduction

In this lesson of Noorani Qaida, you will learn about The Movements (Harakat) in the Arabic language. These movements are short vowel marks that help in proper pronunciation.

Three Types of Movements in Arabic

01 Fatha ( َ ) – Placed above a letter and produces a short "a" sound. Example: بَ → "ba"
02 Kasra ( ِ ) – Placed below a letter and produces a short "i" sound. Example: بِ → "bi"
03 Damma ( ُ ) – Placed above a letter and produces a short "u" sound. Example: بُ → "bu"


How to Pronounce These Movements?


Do not prolong the sound (avoid stretching).
Do not read with a jerk; pronounce smoothly.
By practicing these movements, you will improve your Arabic pronunciation and reading skills.
