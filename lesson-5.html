Noorani Qaida Lesson-5: The Tanween

Introduction

In this lesson of Noorani Qaida, you will learn about Tanween, which consists of double vowel marks used in Arabic pronunciation. You will also practice Ikhfa, an important Tajweed rule.

What is Tanween?

Tanween refers to the double vowel marks added to the end of a noun, indicating an indefinite form. There are three types of Tanween:
01 Double Fatha ( ـً ) – Produces an "an" sound. Example: بً → "ban"
02 Double Kasra ( ـٍ ) – Produces an "in" sound. Example: بٍ → "bin"
03 Double Damma ( ـٌ ) – Produces an "un" sound. Example: بٌ → "bun"

What is Ikhfa?

Ikhfa means hiding the sound of Meem (م) and Noon (ن) partially in the nose when followed by specific letters.

Important Rule:

Do not apply Ikhfa when Tanween is followed by throatal letters (أ, هـ, ع, ح, غ, خ).

Practice for Better Pronunciation

Focus on soft and smooth pronunciation.
Avoid over-emphasizing the nasal sound.
Recognize when to apply Ikhfa and when not to. By practicing this lesson, you will improve your Quranic recitation skills with proper Tajweed.
