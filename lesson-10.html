Exercise of Movement

Introduction

Standing Fatha, Kasra, Damma, and Maddoleen or Tanween In Arabic pronunciation, different types of Madd (prolongation) occur based on the presence of Hamza (ء) or Sukoon ( ْ ) after a Maddah ( ٓ ). Below are the classifications:

Exercise of Movement in Arabic Pronunciation

Types of Madd (Prolongation):

Madd Al-Muttasil (Connected Madd) If Hamza (ء) appears in the same word after a Maddah, it is called Madd Al-Muttasil. Example: جَآءَ Madd Al-Munfasil (Separated Madd) If Hamza (ء) appears at the beginning of the next word, it is called Madd Al-Munfasil. Example: اِنَّآ اَنْزَلْنَا Madd Al-Lazim (Necessary Madd) If Sukoon ( ْ ) follows a Maddah or Leen Letters, the word is prolonged like Madd and is called Madd Al-Lazim. Example: آلْنَٰن Madd Al-Aarid (Casual Madd) If Sukoon ( ْ ) occurs due to a waqf (stop), the Madd is called Madd Al-Aarid. Example: جُوْعٍ
