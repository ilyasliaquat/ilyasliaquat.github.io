Maddah Letters in Arabic

In Arabic, certain letters are prolonged when they follow specific vowel markings. These are known as Maddah Letters and are extended for a duration equal to one Alif or one to two seconds. Alif Maddah (ا): When an Alif (ا) is preceded by a letter with Fatha ( ـَ ), it is called Alif Maddah and is elongated. Waw Maddah (و): When a Waw Sakin (وْ) is preceded by a letter with Damma ( ـُ ), it is called Waw Maddah and is extended. Ya Maddah (ي): When a Ya Sakin (يْ) is preceded by a letter with Kasra ( ـِ ), it is called Ya Maddah and is prolonged. These letters play a crucial role in proper pronunciation and recitation in Arabic.
